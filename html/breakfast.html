VITUMBUA (Mini Rice Cake)

Ingredients
1 cup finely ground rice flour
4 tablespoons plain whole wheat flour
1 packet of coconut milk powder
1 egg white
1/2 cup warm water
1 teaspoon yeast
1/4 cup sugar
1 teaspoon ground cardamom seeds
Oil for frying

Instructions
 Mix rice flour, plain flour, sugar, yeast, cardamom and the coconut powder then add the water slowly to form a thick paste.
Cover and keep in a warm area for 45 minutes (it will double in size and you’ll see bubbles on the top).
Give the batter a stirring.
Heat the appam pan, place a drop of oil in each pan, pour a teaspoonful of batter so that the pan is 3/4 full.
Cook for 2-3 minutes (until it browns), flip the vitumbua.
Cook the other side and repeat until all the batter is finished.

MUHOGO WA NAZI (Cassava in Coconut sauce)

Ingredients
 Cassava
Coconut milk
Salt
Onions

Instructions
 Peel and cut the cassava into pieces, wash and place in a pot.
Add some water and boil the cassava until it is almost tender, remove from the stove and drain the water.
Add coconut milk to cover the cassava.
Add salt, some diced onions and green chili peppers (optional).
Cook until the cassava is completely soft and the coconut milk has reduced.
Remove the pot from the stove and keep aside.
Now take a small pot and add heavy coconut milk and cook it until it thickens, then pour it over the cassava.
Remove the cassava and serve it in a bowl.

MAHAMRI

Ingredients
 3 cups all-purpose/plain flour
3/4 teaspoon instant yeast
7 tablespoon sugar
1 teaspoon ground cardamom
3/4 cup heavy coconut milk
light coconut milk (½ - ¾ cup)
Oil for deep frying

Instructions
 Sieve the flour, add yeast, sugar and cardamom. Start kneading with the coconut milk adding a little at a time until you have a soft, firm but not sticky dough.
Divide the dough into two and shape into balls. Let them stand for an hour to rise. When ready (have risen), place each ball on a floured surface.
Divide each ball in half again, form into a smooth ball, roll each ball into a circle of quarter inch thick, divide each circle into 4 sections. Repeat with the rest of the balls.
Place the shaped dough on a lightly floured surface and keep covered for an hour to rise.
Deep fry the dough, splashing oil over them to help them puff up. When they start puffing up, turn them over.
Fry until golden brown in colour. Repeat until all are done.

UJI WA NGANO (Whole Wheat Grain Porridge)

Ingredients
 5 cups milk
1 Small Cup Sugar
2 cups coconut milk
Ground cardamom
3 table spoons wheat flour
3 cups boiled wheat seeds

Instructions
 Pour the milk in a pot together with coconut milk, sugar, ground cardamom and cook.
In a bowl mix the flour and 3/4 to 1 cup of water.
Once the milk boils, add the flour and water mixture and stir to avoid any lumps. The porridge will start to thicken.
Cook for a about 5 minutes then add the cooked wheat.
Continue cooking and stirring on low heat so that the porridge doesn’t stick to the pot.
Check to see if the porridge is thick enough, if it’s not, you can add more (flour and water) mixture.
Add sugar to taste.
You can use 1.5 litres of water instead of milk in the first step and add bring to boil before adding the coconut mixture and other ingredients.
Remove the porridge from the stove when everything is well mixed and the consistency is to your liking.
Serve hot.
