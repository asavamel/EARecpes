PILAU

Ingredients
 ½ kg meat
1 cup rice
2 onions chopped
2 potatoes peeled and chopped in 4 pieces
½ teaspoon turmeric
Oil to fry
2 teaspoon garlic paste
1 teaspoon chopped coriander
1 teaspoon grated onion
½ teaspoon of ginger
A bit of pepper
1 tablespoon pilau masala (ground cumin, cardamom, cinnamon, pepper, cloves)

Instructions
 Boil the meat with 4 to 5 cups of water, 1 teaspoon of ginger, 1 teaspoon garlic paste, pepper and grated onion.
Wash rice thoroughly and soak for 30 minutes.
Once the meat is ready, drain it and set aside. Reserve the soup for cooking the rice.
Heat oil in a large cooking pan. Add pilau masala. After 30 seconds add chopped onions. Fry them until they are translucent stirring with a wooden spoon.
Add the potatoes, then add turmeric powder. Fry this for a few minutes and add the garlic and ginger paste.
Add a bit of chopped coriander and the boiled meat and stir for a few minutes. Add the stock and some salt, let the soup simmer.
When the water starts boiling, drain your soaked rice and add it to the stock. Stir a little and sprinkle a bit of coriander on top.
Cover well and let the rice cook on low medium heat.
When there’s very little water left in the rice, reduce the heat to the lowest point. Cover the pan tightly and leave the rice to continue steaming for 10 minutes.
Serve your pilau with vegetable salad.

VIAZI KARAI (Potatoes in gram flour)

Ingredients:
 2 lbs potatoes (boiled and peeled)
Oil for frying
1 ½ cups gram flour
2 cups wheat flour
Salt
Chilli powder
Lemon juice
Pinch of yellow food color

Instructions
 Mix your salt. chilli powder and lemon juice to make a thick paste.
Cut the potatoes into half, spread the chilli paste and join the halves together and then cut them into halves or if the potatoes are big into quarters.
Mix the gram flour and wheat flour with water to make a thick batter, add some salt in the batter to taste.
On a medium high stove, place your frying vessel and add oil.
Once oil is hot, place your potatoes in the batter and start frying. Do not over fill the vessel.
Turn them to cook the other side. When both sides are cooked, remove from heat and drain excess oil.
Serve with chutney.

SAMAKI WA KUPAKA (Fish in Coconut sauce)

Ingredients
 2 sliced fish
2 chopped tomatoes
2 onions
Cooking oil
Coconut milk: -1 cup heavy milk -1 cup light milk
1tablespoon mashed garlic
1tablespoon mashed ginger
3 green chillies
Freshly chopped coriander
2tablespoon turmeric powder
1tea spoon ground black paper
2 medium sized lemons
Salt

Instructions
 For the marinate mix:
 1 tablespoon of turmeric powder.
1tsp ground black pepper.
1 tablespoon of mashed garlic and ginger.
2 chopped green chillies.
2 tablespoon lemon juice.
1 tablespoon cooking oil.
Salt to taste.
Marinate the fish with the mixture and set aside for 30mins.
Put the marinated fish on a grill and cook both sides over medium heat until tender then set aside to cool.
For the fish curry:
Blend chopped tomatoes together with the chopped onion, coriander, 1 green chilli, 1 tablespoon of turmeric powder, salt to taste and 1 cup of light coconut milk.
Cook the mixture until it thickens.
Add the 1 cup of heavy coconut milk to the thickened mixture and stir.
Let the curry simmer for 2mins.
Add 1 tablespoon of lemon juice and stir gently.
Remove from heat then smear over the marinated fish.
